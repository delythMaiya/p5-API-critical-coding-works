**RESEARCHING**
My takeaway from each listed works:     
**Emoter (Tim Hawkinson)** -   I really like the idea of how he mapped the lightness of TV screen to each different motors which when combined creates an organic evolving motion to the face, and using TV screen was an idea I liked also as people are fixated on the screen and it felt like it can also be a work to mock how people get fixated onto the TV screen. I personally would be too afraid to use my own face.  

**Reface** - I like the element of audience participation and how the work can be controlled by our motion like blinking too. also, the automatic alightment is very seamless and works effective. However, it doesn't allow the audience to ask if they want to be in the work, if someone just wanted to observe the work - not to make exaggerated facial expressions in front of everyone- it's not really for them. If I were to create something with audience participation, I personally would want to create something that is not in-your face directly asking to be.   

**Stranger Visions** - I found it fascinating and very creepy at the same time - which is why I liked it. It paints the idea of how advanced the surveillance system has become and how much a left-behind DNA print can identify. I do wonder though - how accurate this is to the actual person's face and it also doesn't give us the idea of hair style of tattoos or makeup that would change the impression a lot. But for purpose of art to tell the message of surveiillance and technology, i think it is very effective.   

Memories of Passersby I - I like the idea of using old portraits - copyright free material to train the neural network with his own prefeerence. It makes me wonder how the output would change in 10 or 50 years time if it keeps evolving and continoisly trains itself. I resemble this work with Brian Eno's installation works featuring faces (https://factoryinternational.org/whats-on/brian-eno-face-to-face/) and I resonate with it a lot. 

**Advertising Positions** - I love how personal this is for every person taking part in this. I often think about the enormous amount of advertisements and information that bombard us in our daily life, trying to divert our attention and time as much as possible. When faced with this art work, especially combined with facial expression of a person from the 3D scan, it felt more threatening to realise how submerged we are in this.   

**Facial Weaponization Suite** - I liked how 'facelessness' was linked to escape from the system by not making our faces available to any algorithms. This work strongly relates to the reading we did, of how code is never neutral. 

**Responsive Face** - After trying every parameter to see how the face expression shifts, I turned on the random mode on, and the results were interesting. The shift between expressions are smoothed out, and it seemed like the face was going through a circular pattern, particularly mouth. It would go from O shape to A shape and back to O again. If Perlin noise is used as the algorithm here, it makes sense how the shifts between expressions are smoothed out.
